What is the National Defense Service Medal Decoration?

The National Defense Service Medal is awarded to anyone has served on active duty in the United States Armed Forced during either the Korean War, Vietnam War, Persian Gulf War, and the Global War on Terrorism. This award can be awarded to Reserve members if they are in good standing even though they are not on active duty. Unlike other awards, Cadets and midshipmen may receive it even if they have yet to graduate their schools. Each additional award is signified by a bronze star and can only be granted per different conflict period mentioned above.

The United States military sorts decorations into categories called Classes and Types. The National Defense Service Medal medal is classified as a Service Award of the type General Service Medal.

How Do You Earn the National Defense Service Medal?

This medal is awarded for serving honorably in either the Korean War, Vietnam War, Persian Gulf War, and the Global War on Terrorism. Each additional award is signified by a bronze star and can only be granted per different conflict period mentioned above.
